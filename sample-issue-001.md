📋 **Quick Wins**

**This week's copy-paste prompt:**

> *"I need to give feedback to a colleague who missed a deadline. They do great work but reliability is becoming an issue. Draft a message that's direct but preserves the relationship. Keep it under 150 words."*

Try it in ChatGPT or Claude. The AI won't nail your voice, but it'll give you a starting point that's 10x better than staring at a blank email.

**One more:**

> *"Summarize this meeting transcript into: (1) decisions made, (2) action items with owners, (3) topics we should revisit next time."*

If your video call tool doesn't auto-summarize yet, paste the transcript into any AI tool with this prompt.

🔍 **What Leaders Need to Know**

[**Your team is probably using AI already — and not telling you (3 minute read)**](VERIFY-URL)
Multiple surveys now show that employees hide AI use from managers because they're afraid of looking lazy or getting in trouble. The fix isn't a ban. It's clear guidelines: what's allowed, what needs disclosure, and what's off-limits. If you haven't had this conversation with your team, this week is a good time.

[**The data privacy trap in "personal" AI accounts (3 minute read)**](VERIFY-URL)
Here's what most teams get wrong: it's not free vs. paid — it's personal vs. business. Personal accounts on ChatGPT and Claude (including paid Plus, Pro, and Max plans) can use your conversations for model training unless you find the setting and turn it off. Business plans like ChatGPT Team and Claude for Work don't train on your data, period. If anyone on your team pastes client data or internal strategy into a personal account, that's your gap. A business plan at $25-30/user/month is the cheapest insurance you'll buy.
